Patintero Arose during the Spanish Colonization period, the Patintero is played by mostly children in the streets of the Philippines.The Spanish term "patear," which means "to kick," and the Tagalog word "tero," which means "guard," are thought to be the origins of the game. The players will split themselves into two teams, one for defenders and one for runners. The runners will try and get to across while trying not to get caught or tagged by the defenders. If a runner is tagged, they're out of the game. Otherwise, they earn a point. The game ends when everyone gets tagged.
Sungka Sungka is known all over Southeast Asia, even carrying different names depending on the coutnry (congkak - Malaysia, dakon - Indonesia and Brunei, etc). It originates from the pre-colonial era and is now a part of our history. It's believed to be introduced to the Philippines by Malay settlers who used seashells as playing pieces The players will take turns in picking up the shells from one of their holes and disperse it in a counterclockwise way. if the last shell wil land in a hole with an even number of shels, the player goes again. The player with the maniest shells in their house wins.
Tumbang Preso A traditional game that is played by majority children outdoors. The name is derived from the Filipino word, Tumba, which means fall, and preso, which means prisoner. By tossing the slipper on the line and determining which player is the furthest away, the game determines who will be the guard of the can. The other players will try and hit the can again with a slipper, if they knock it down, they can retrieve it. The tagger should run to put the can in the circle and tag one of the players.
Luksong Baka Originated in Bulacan, Luksong baka directly translates to jump over the cow. This being the main goal of the game The main goal is to jump over the person, called the baka, without touching or falling over.
Piko Piko is the Filipino version of the childhood game, hopscothc. The players take turns in throwing a stone or any marker at the center and hopping from each part of the drawn platform without touching the lines.
